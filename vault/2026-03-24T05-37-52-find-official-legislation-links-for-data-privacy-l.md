# Find official legislation links for data privacy laws in the United States, specifically for CCPA and its equivalents at the state level, which serve similar purposes to GDPR within the US context.

In the United States, several states have enacted comprehensive data privacy laws that serve purposes similar to the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The most prominent among these is the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), which was later amended and expanded by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA). Beyond California, states like Virginia, Colorado, Utah, Connecticut, Delaware, Indiana, and Texas have also passed their own significant privacy legislation.

Here are official and authoritative links to the legislation for these key data privacy laws:

### California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)

The CCPA, enacted in 2018 and effective January 1, 2020, was the first comprehensive state data privacy law in the U.S.. It grants California consumers various rights over their personal information, including the right to know what data businesses collect about them, the right to delete personal information, and the right to opt-out of the sale of their personal information.

In November 2020, California voters approved Proposition 24, the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), which amended and expanded the CCPA, introducing new additional privacy protections that began on January 1, 2023.

### Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA)

Virginia's Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA) was signed into law on March 2, 2021, and became effective on January 1, 2023. The VCDPA grants Virginia residents rights to access, correct, delete, and obtain copies of their personal data, and to opt-out of the processing of personal data for targeted advertising, the sale of personal data, or profiling.

*   **Code of Virginia - Chapter 53. Consumer Data Protection Act:**
    *   [Link to VCDPA on Virginia Law Library of Virginia](https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacodefull/title59.1/chapter53/)

### Colorado Privacy Act (CPA)

The Colorado Privacy Act (CPA) was signed into law on July 7, 2021, and became effective on July 1, 2023. It provides Colorado consumers with rights to access, delete, and correct their personal data, as well as the right to opt-out of the sale of their personal data or its use for targeted advertising or certain types of profiling.

*   **Colorado General Assembly - Senate Bill 21-190 (Protect Personal Data Privacy):** This is the enacted bill that established the CPA.
    *   [Link to SB21-190 on Colorado General Assembly](https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb21-190)
*   **Colorado Attorney General - Colorado Privacy Act (CPA) Page:** Provides information and guidance on the CPA and its regulations.
    *   [Link to Colorado Attorney General's CPA Page](https://coag.gov/consumer-protection/data-privacy-and-cyber-security/colorado-privacy-act/)

### Utah Consumer Privacy Act (UCPA)

The Utah Consumer Privacy Act (UCPA) was signed into law in March 2022 and became effective on December 31, 2023. The UCPA provides consumers with rights to access, delete, and obtain a copy of their personal data, and to opt-out of the processing of personal data for targeted advertising or the sale of personal data.

*   **Utah Legislature - Chapter 61 Utah Consumer Privacy Act:**
    *   [Link to UCPA on Utah Legislature](https://le.utah.gov/xcode/Title13/Chapter61/13-61.html)

### Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA)

The Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA) was signed into law on May 10, 2022, and went into effect on July 1, 2023. The CTDPA grants Connecticut residents rights to access, correct, and delete their personal data, and to opt-out of the sale of their personal data, targeted advertising, and certain profiling.

*   **Connecticut General Assembly - Public Act No. 22-15 (An Act Concerning Personal Data Privacy and Online Monitoring):** This is the legislation for the CTDPA.
    *   [Link to CTDPA on Connecticut General Assembly (Public Act No. 22-15)](https://www.cga.ct.gov/2022/ACT/PA/PDF/2022PA-00015-R00SB-00006-PA.PDF)
*   **CT.gov - The Connecticut Data Privacy Act:** Provides an overview and key provisions of the CTDPA.
    *   [Link to CT.gov CTDPA Page](https://portal.ct.gov/AG/Civil-Liberties/The-Connecticut-Data-Privacy-Act)

### Delaware Personal Data Privacy Act (DPDPA)

The Delaware Personal Data Privacy Act (DPDPA) was signed into law on September 11, 2023, and became effective on January 1, 2025. The DPDPA gives Delaware residents rights to know what information is being collected about them, to see, correct inaccuracies, and request deletion of their personal data.

*   **LegiScan - Delaware House Bill 154 (Latest Bill Text):** This link provides the engrossed text of the bill that created the DPDPA.
    *   [Link to DE HB154 on LegiScan](https://legis.delaware.gov/BillDetail?legislationId=132715)

### Indiana Consumer Data Protection Act (INCDPA)

The Indiana Consumer Data Protection Act (INCDPA) was passed in 2023 and will be enforced starting January 1, 2026. This law gives Hoosiers the right to understand how their data is used, make informed choices about its use, sale, or sharing, and request its deletion or correction.

*   **Indiana General Assembly - IC 24-15 Article 15. Consumer Data Protection:**
    *   [Link to INCDPA on Indiana General Assembly](https://iga.in.gov/legislative/2023/bills/house/0005/#document-ed2b8d00)

### Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA)

The Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA) was enacted on June 18, 2023, and became effective on July 1, 2024. The Act grants Texas residents several key rights over their personal data, including the right to know whether a company is processing their personal data, to obtain it in a readable format, to correct inaccuracies, to delete personal data, and to opt-out of the processing of personal data for targeted advertising, sale, or profiling.
